Emergent Constraints:
different climate models, give different answers about future climate
emergent constraints can be used to narrow the range of answers

basic concept: analyze large ensemble of climate model -> clear relationship emerges between variable X
	in current simulated climate and different variable Y in model's projection of future.
	when relationship tested in various ways -> plug in actual observations of variable X to narrow 
	the range of answers for variable Y.
reduce uncertainties -> identify emp relationship between inter-model spread of observable A and
	inter-model responses B
graphic: linear relationship
	weight different models according to oberserved measure fit
criteria:1.understanding of physical relationship
	 2.correct use of observations, uncertainties not too large, obervations should be available on 
		long time scale
	 3.relevance of constrained climate-change projections
		usually only correlation coefficients
		-> ? does number of climate models and/or level of structural difference matter
